# Predictive History Scenario Board Seed

User direction: build genuinely unique speculative-history and predictive-history assets that feel like cinematic analytical boards rather than generic school posters. They should explain deeply while preserving a tense, thesis-driven visual identity.

## Priority Users

- Video essayists adapting forecasting or speculative-history theses into visual packages.
- Documentary editors who need reusable pressure maps, timeline boards, and doctrine sheets.
- Teachers and self-learners who want dense visual summaries of a geopolitical or civilizational argument.
- Indie filmmakers and worldbuilders borrowing strategic-history visual language for story development.
- Agents retrieving structured visual evidence for historical analogy, scenario comparison, and forecast framing.

## Required Run Shape

- Produce `10` final PNGs minimum.
- Use at least `3` visual families.
- Keep one family strongly explanatory, one cinematic, and one systems-oriented.

## Desired Asset Families

- Scenario board for one predictive-history thesis.
- Pressure map showing actors, resources, chokepoints, and strategic vectors.
- Doctrine sheet comparing rival strategic logics.
- Timeline board with turning points, forks, and counterfactual pressure.
- Civilizational dashboard with variables, factions, risks, and horizon markers.
- Historical analogy comparison plate.
- Escalation tree or decision tree.
- Propaganda or legitimacy comparison board.
- Strategic geography map with annotations and symbols.
- Cinematic proof frame that shows the same analytical language working as visual storytelling.

## Visual Rules

- Avoid generic classroom-poster composition.
- Use a war-room, dossier, doctrine-sheet, or prestige-documentary visual language.
- Keep labels short and evidentiary rather than loud and decorative.
- Ensure the image still communicates even with most text removed.
- Treat every board as a reusable production asset, not just an infographic.
