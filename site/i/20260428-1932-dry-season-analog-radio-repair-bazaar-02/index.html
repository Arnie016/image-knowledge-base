Composition Notes

The lineup needed enough spacing to read each role while still feeling embedded in the bazaar.

Prompt Extract

Use case: photorealistic-natural
Asset type: fashion documentary lineup
Primary request: a lineup of six analog radio repair bazaar workers and regulars standing under patched canvas awnings in a dry-season market lane
Scene/background: narrow repair alley with hanging antennas, stacked radio shells, chalk price boards, fan cages, and shelves of spare knobs behind them
Subject: veteran tuner with magnifying visor, teenage errand runner with frequency cards, middle-aged coil rewinder in a dust-stained apron, rooftop antenna installer with canvas harness, warranty clerk with stamp tin, and an elderly customer carrying a repaired set wrapped in cloth
Style/medium: documentary editorial photography, human and unsentimental, not fashion-glamorous
Composition/framing: full-body straight-on group portrait with each figure clearly readable and spaced, market context visible but secondary
Lighting/mood: severe white noon light broken by shade-cloth shadows, dry and practical
Color palette: sun-faded teal, tobacco brown, chalk beige, oxidized copper, faded maroon, dull silver
Materials/textures: washed cotton, canvas aprons, leather straps, brass clips, dusty Bakelite, stamped paper cards
Constraints: no logos; no watermark; no futuristic gadgets; no luxury styling; keep clothing patched, affordable, and work-driven
Avoid: runway posing; glossy streetwear editorial; cute nostalgia; fake
