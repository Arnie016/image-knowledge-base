Composition Notes

Reflection-heavy foreground and layered terraces were the key devices for making the procession feel embedded in agricultural infrastructure.

Prompt Extract

Use case: photorealistic-natural
Asset type: narrative documentary still
Primary request: a monsoon wedding procession crossing a series of flooded rice terraces at blue hour, carrying bamboo lanterns and food parcels between narrow raised paths
Scene/background: layered rice terraces reflecting storm clouds, distant village lights, bamboo gate markers, low mist, wet stone steps
Subject: procession of relatives in indigo rain capes, bride and partner under a woven lantern canopy, musicians, cooks, elders, children, baskets of herbs and rice, path workers adjusting lantern poles
Style/medium: wide documentary photography with cinematic restraint, real weather, no fantasy glow
Composition/framing: wide-angle scene with strong foreground reflections, midground procession, deep terrace layers receding into mist
Lighting/mood: blue-hour storm light with warm lantern pockets, tender communal energy, recent rain visible on every surface
Color palette: deep indigo, muted gold, wet green, clay brown, cloud silver, warm amber lantern light
Materials/textures: water reflections, soaked fabric, bamboo poles, muddy earth, woven baskets, paper lantern skin
Constraints: no logos; no watermark; no fireworks; no palace architecture; keep scale human and agricultural, not parade-spectacle
Avoid: tourist postcard clichés; fantasy temple skyline; neon color; dry weather; empty terraces
