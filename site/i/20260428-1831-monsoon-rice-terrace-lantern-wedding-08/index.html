Test whether a quiet aftermath frame can close a ceremonial run without needing comic narration or spectacle.

Composition Notes

The pavilion is kept distant so the recovery chores and water reflections carry the emotional closure.

Prompt Extract

Use case: photorealistic-natural
Asset type: dawn aftermath landscape still
Primary request: the morning after the wedding, relatives walk the rice terraces collecting lanterns and folded cloth from wet bamboo poles while fog lifts off the paddies
Scene/background: broad stepped terraces after rain, pale dawn sky, low drifting mist, scattered lantern reflections, distant cooking smoke
Subject: three generations gathering extinguished lanterns, rolling cloth canopies, stacking bamboo frames, carrying leftover herb bundles and empty tea tins, with the pavilion now quiet in the distance
Style/medium: photoreal landscape documentary, gentle realism, no melodrama
Composition/framing: wide horizontal frame with foreground water reflections, midground workers, and distant pavilion silhouette for closure
Lighting/mood: cool dawn light with faint warm remnants from a few unextinguished lanterns, restful and communal
Color palette: pale gray-blue sky, wet green terraces, indigo cloth, faded amber lanterns, clay brown earth, cream mist
Materials/textures: standing water, mud, bamboo, soaked cloth, paper lantern skin, tin, reed baskets
Constraints: no logos; no watermark; no disaster feeling; no empty cinematic emptiness; keep it human, agricultural, and quietly celebratory
Avoid: dramatic storm apocalypse; tourist brochure gloss; fantasy sunrise beams; abandoned-event sadness
