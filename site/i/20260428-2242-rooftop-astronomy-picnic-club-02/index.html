Test whether a fictional community can become recognizable through repeated practical props and layered silhouettes without turning into a brand campaign.

Composition Notes

The straight-on lineup keeps the carried tools doing the differentiation work while the slight spacing irregularity preserves documentary plausibility.

Prompt Extract

Use case: photorealistic-natural
Primary request: a documentary-style rooftop club portrait lineup of six astronomy picnic regulars preparing for a public sky lesson
Scene/background: simple roof wall and low city glow at blue night with folded blankets, thermoses, and paper maps in hand
Subject: six distinct adults in layered practical cold-weather clothing, each carrying one recognizable skywatch object such as binoculars, star wheel, red flashlight, thermos, or sketchbook
Style/medium: photoreal editorial group portrait, believable public-night community scene, not fashion advertising
Composition/framing: full-body lineup with slight spacing variations, straight-on camera, enough room to read silhouettes and carried tools
Lighting/mood: soft blue-hour night with gentle tungsten spill from a stairwell door and restrained grain
Color palette: navy wool, rust red, cream paper, soot gray, brass, muted plaid accents
Materials/textures: knit scarves, canvas satchels, wool coats, enamel cups, brass optics, paper charts
Constraints: no logos; no glamor pose language; original fictional community only; no watermark; practical clothing over styled costumes
Avoid: luxury outerwear campaign; sci-fi uniforms; festival costumes; branded streetwear; exaggerated cosplay energy
