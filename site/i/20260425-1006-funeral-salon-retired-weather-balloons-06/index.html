Test whether a memorial-object family can feel native to the ceremonial institution rather than an external merch drop.

Composition Notes

The family needed spacing and tiering like a museum counter, with enough softness to keep the cloud boxes and swatch vials from becoming hard retail product shots.

Prompt Extract

Use case: product-mockup
Asset type: keepsake family for a funeral salon archive gift counter
Primary request: a restrained family of memorial keepsakes made from retired weather balloon materials
Scene/background: stone and dark felt display surface in a quiet museum shop corner
Subject: small lacquered cloud boxes, brass valve medallions, ribbon-wrapped vials holding tiny latex swatches, stamped condolence seals, weather-map postcards, black enamel pins, folded pennants, and one boxed patch-repair kit presented as a coherent family
Style/medium: premium catalog photography with documentary restraint
Composition/framing: three-quarter tabletop family portrait, varied heights, clear spacing, no hands
Lighting/mood: soft directional museum light, intimate and tactile
Color palette: charcoal, indigo, ivory, oxidized brass, faded signal orange as a small accent
Materials/textures: lacquer, enamel, brass, waxed paper, ribbon, worn latex, linen boxes
Constraints: quiet collectible objects rather than luxury merch; no readable brand names; no logos; no watermark
Avoid: glossy lifestyle ad, jewelry glamour, wedding favors, toy packaging, souvenir kitsch
