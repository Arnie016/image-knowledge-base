Learning Value

Test whether a retail scene can make the brand feel embedded in public transit culture instead of collapsing into hospitality or ad-shot tropes.

Composition Notes

The kiosk stays dominant while the boarding gate and harbor remain readable as supporting civic context.

Prompt Extract

Use case: photorealistic-natural
Asset type: public retail environment still
Primary request: a bright midday ferry-terminal kiosk dedicated to a fictional kelp-and-citrus soda launch, with refrigerated coolers, stacked carrier crates, hand-painted price slats, route-color shelf strips, and commuters buying cold bottles before boarding
Scene/background: open-air commuter ferry terminal with turnstiles, ticket stanchions, harbor railings, gull-shadow reflections, and a boat ramp glimpsed beyond
Subject: a modest, practical beverage kiosk that feels local and heavily used, not luxury retail, with soda packaging and paper ephemera integrated into the space
Style/medium: documentary editorial photography
Composition/framing: eye-level wide shot, kiosk centered, visible foreground cooler and midground customer interaction, skyline secondary
Lighting/mood: crisp saline daylight, wind-bright highlights, natural color, subtle film grain
Color palette: cream paint, oxidized teal metal, citrus yellow labels, galvanized steel, navy vinyl, safety-orange striping
Materials/textures: chilled glass, brushed metal, painted wood, wet concrete, paper tickets, corrugated boxes, condensation, sun-faded awning fabric
Constraints: no real brand marks, no watermark, no giant readable menu text, no beach-party styling, no futuristic screens
Avoid: boutique cafe chic, luxury resort mood, surfer stereot
