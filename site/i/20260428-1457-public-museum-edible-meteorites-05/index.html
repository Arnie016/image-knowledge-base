Prompt Extract

Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: architectural section cutaway
Primary request: a richly detailed cross-section of the Public Museum of Edible Meteorites, showing the noon atrium, tasting classroom, specimen vault, rooftop crater garden, small cafe, family route stair, and compact gift shop as one coherent public institution
Scene/background: full building section on a pale neutral background, with surrounding sidewalk and planted forecourt visible at the base and a simple sky band above
Subject: an educational museum architecture built from limestone, terrazzo, brushed aluminum, celadon panels, amber canopy inserts, and charcoal specimen displays, with edible meteorite exhibits integrated into public circulation
Style/medium: architectural illustration plate with realistic materials and tidy cutaway logic
Composition/framing: straight-on section cut, many rooms visible at once, clear vertical stacking, strong circulation path, browsable detail density
Lighting/mood: noon daylight entering from skylights and facade openings, bright and public-minded
Color palette: limestone cream, terrazzo gray, celadon, amber, charcoal, restrained orange markers
Materials/textures: cut stone, terrazzo, steel rails, paper labels, planted roof soil, glass vitrines, tasting counters
