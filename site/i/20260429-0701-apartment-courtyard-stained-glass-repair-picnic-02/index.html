Prove that the branch can live in an ordinary lived-in housing block and still hold a strong color-and-material identity.

Composition Notes

Lunch plates and tool clutter in the foreground helped the scene stay communal and unscripted instead of drifting into hero-shot restoration photography.

Prompt Extract

Use case: photorealistic-natural
Asset type: image knowledge base final PNG
Primary request: candid documentary photo of a shared apartment courtyard where residents are repairing a stained-glass window during a communal midday picnic
Scene/background: sunlit inner courtyard with chipped stucco walls, rusted balcony rails, laundry lines, folding chairs, sawhorses, enamel plates, and open stair doors
Subject: three to five adults in worn aprons and cotton shirts carefully handling colored-glass panels on padded trestles while neighbors pour lemonade and pass tools, one child only in the distant background and not a focal point
Style/medium: photorealistic documentary photography, ordinary housing block realism, not lifestyle advertising
Composition/framing: eye-level wide shot with foreground tools and lunch plates, midground repair action, upper balconies catching reflected color
Lighting/mood: hard noon sunlight softened by hanging laundry, warm and communal without sentimentality
Color palette: bottle green, amber, chalk white, faded brick red, dusty denim, concrete beige, pale laundry blue
Materials/textures: chipped stucco, oxidized railings, cracked tile, wrinkled aprons, tacky putty, scuffed glass edges, sweating lemonade jar
Constraints: no logos, no branded packaging, no cathedral setting, no wedding mood, no dense readable signs
Avoid: stock-photo smiles, glossy lifest
