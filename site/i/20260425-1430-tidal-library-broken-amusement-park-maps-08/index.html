Use case: illustration-story
Asset type: silent comic page
Primary request: six-panel silent comic page about a librarian recovering a storm-flooded drawer of broken amusement-park maps, drying the fragments, tracing the old promenade route, projecting the restored map at night, and leaving a repaired chart under glass for the next morning
Scene/background: same seaside map library world across panels, with drawer room, repair bench, seawall projection terrace, and reading table
Subject: one practical archivist and the damaged maps; no speech balloons, no caption boxes, storytelling carried by gesture, tools, water, and paper
Style/medium: watercolor-and-ink comic page with tactile paper texture
Composition/framing: six uneven but orderly panels on one page, clear action progression, one larger final panel, readable silhouettes, no panel overcrowding
Lighting/mood: storm gray to lamplit evening to quiet morning, reflective and hopeful, not sentimental
Color palette: indigo gray, sea-glass teal, cream paper, muted coral, brass amber
Materials/textures: wet paper fibers, ink wash, tissue repairs, wood drawers, window rain, projection glow
Constraints: no text; no logos; no watermark; quiet documentary storytelling, not superhero comics, not manga exaggeration, not children's-book cuteness
