Prompt Extract

Use case: typographic-layout
Asset type: printed concert program and notation ephemera suite
Primary request: a top-down editorial still life of a river conservatory performance packet including a folded concert program, strip-like notation tabs, seat token, wax-sealed envelope, indigo ribbon marker, and a few handwritten rehearsal cards
Scene/background: arranged on a pale limestone table with one river pebble paperweight and one brass clip, treated as tactile printed matter rather than a flat mockup
Subject: a coherent family of school concert ephemera with minimal short text and musical symbols
Style/medium: museum-shop editorial stationery photography, restrained, tactile, public-institution rather than luxury branding
Composition/framing: square top-down arrangement with one partly opened foldout program and layered tabs creating depth
Lighting/mood: soft afternoon daylight, archival, orderly, slightly worn
Color palette: cream, indigo, charcoal, brass, shell white, muted green
Materials/textures: textured paper, soot-black ink, vellum strips, cotton ribbon, sealing wax, brass token
Text (verbatim): "RIVER SONGS"
Constraints: text should be minimal and non-critical, rely on icons, numerals, and notation fragments more than paragraphs, no logos, no watermark
Avoid: glossy brochure styling, modern concert branding, fantasy scrolls, perfectly clean packaging, clutter
