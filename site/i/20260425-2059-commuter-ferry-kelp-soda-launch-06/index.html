Use case: photorealistic-natural
Asset type: launch-day documentary still
Primary request: commuters on the open upper deck of a working city ferry drinking a fictional kelp-and-citrus soda during its launch week, with windblown jackets, carrier crates, ticket stubs, and wake spray catching sunlight
Scene/background: active daytime harbor crossing with skyline, railings, painted deck lanes, bench seating, and a boarding ramp receding behind
Subject: ordinary commuters and launch staff sharing cold bottles and cans in motion, proving the brand works as lived public transit culture rather than staged advertising
Style/medium: candid documentary photo
Composition/framing: medium-wide eye-level frame, people grouped naturally, soda visible but not dominant, skyline secondary, no posed smiles
Lighting/mood: bright marine daylight with real contrast, salt haze, subtle grain, honest energy
Color palette: navy outerwear, cream labels, silver cans, sea-glass greens, orange route accents, harbor blue-gray
Materials/textures: vinyl seats, painted steel deck, wet shoes, paper tickets, glass bottles, aluminum cans, windbreaker nylon, rope crate handles
Constraints: no real ferry logos, no watermark, no luxury-yacht vibe, no influencer posing, no giant signage
Avoid: tourism brochure staging, beachwear, sports-team tailgate energy, cyberpunk port lighting, stock-photo perfection
