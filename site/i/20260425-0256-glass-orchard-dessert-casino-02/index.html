Learning Value

Test whether costume and service attire can expand the corpus into fashion-adjacent territory while staying disciplined, social, and tied to the building's material world.

Composition Notes

Six evenly spaced full-body figures with subtle pose changes and trays or books as secondary props.

Prompt Extract

Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: fashion lineup for a fictional hospitality-and-gaming world
Primary request: six pastry dealers and tasting hosts from the Glass Orchard Dessert Casino standing in a calm editorial lineup, showing coordinated uniforms with aprons, cuffed gloves, citrus-branch pins, satin waist ties, terrazzo shoes, and translucent sugar-chip accessories
Scene/background: clean pale backdrop with a faint greenhouse haze, no props except a few held trays and brass token books
Subject: a disciplined costume family that feels service-oriented, theatrical, and believable, not like runway couture and not like fantasy cosplay
Style/medium: high-end editorial fashion photograph with crisp realism and soft textile detail
Composition/framing: full-body straight-on lineup, evenly spaced figures, subtle pose variation, clear silhouettes, one wide frame
Lighting/mood: bright diffused daylight through greenhouse glass, poised, fresh, sociable
Color palette: cream, pistachio, apricot, smoked amber, brushed brass, pale coral accents
Materials/textures: linen aprons, matte silk waist ties, velvet trim, translucent resin buttons, polished brass pins, sugar-glass jewelry
Constraints: no logos, no watermark, no playing cards, no bunny suits, no cabaret styling, no living-artist imitation
Avoid: fashion-week drama, sequined showgirl costumes, cyberpunk neon, gothic casino vibe
