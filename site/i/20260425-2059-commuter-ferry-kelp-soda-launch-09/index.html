Composition Notes

The six-panel grid keeps the story readable while the tray-liner wear preserves the feeling of handled transit ephemera.

Prompt Extract

Use case: illustration-story
Asset type: silent tray-liner comic page
Primary request: a six-panel wordless comic printed on a paper tray liner for a fictional commuter-ferry kelp soda launch, showing a commuter buying a bottle at the terminal, carrying it aboard, sharing it on deck, and finishing the trip with the empty bottle tucked into recycling
Scene/background: ferry terminal and open deck rendered as a simple repeating public-transit world with route-color accents
Subject: quiet everyday commuter behavior around the soda, emphasizing motion, exchange, and public reuse rather than advertisement
Style/medium: clean ink-and-flat-color comic with tactile tray-paper texture and slight print misregistration
Composition/framing: six rectangular panels in a tidy grid, readable actions, no speech balloons, no captions
Lighting/mood: bright breezy daytime, friendly but unsentimental
Color palette: cream paper, navy linework, oxidized teal, sea-glass green, citrus yellow, small orange accents
Materials/textures: uncoated paper, offset print texture, slight fold marks, soft wear at edges
Constraints: no text, no real logos, no watermark, no mascot character, must read like printed ephemera not a glossy comic-book cover
Avoid: superhero posing, manga speed lines, children's-book cuteness overload, retro advertising pinup mood, cyberpunk transit
