Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: atlas contact sheet for a speculative bathhouse worldbook
Primary request: a dense catalog-style atlas of ritual bathing objects arranged on a warm stone tabletop, featuring carved mineral basins, cedar ladles, brass locker tokens, folded indigo towels, salt jars, pumice brushes, ceramic scent burners, moon-shaped soap dishes, and small lantern markers from a fictional cliffside mineral bathhouse
Scene/background: straight-down tabletop studio composition on travertine and pale grout with subtle dampness and a few scattered grains of mineral salt
Subject: 24 to 30 distinct bathhouse artifacts with coherent design language, all original and buildable, grouped in tidy families with breathing room
Style/medium: tactile catalog photography with editorial restraint, premium hospitality objects, believable materials, no fantasy props
Composition/framing: top-down contact sheet, evenly lit, dense but organized, every object fully visible, no single hero object dominating
Lighting/mood: soft overhead daylight with gentle shadow falloff, quiet ceremonial mood
Color palette: limestone cream, indigo cloth, cedar brown, oxidized brass, chalk white salt, pale mineral green
Materials/textures: wet stone, matte ceramic glaze, brushed brass, woven cotton, raw cedar, granular salt, pumice, soft steam residue
Constraints: no real brands, no logos, no copyri
