Composition Notes

The top-down grid resists hero-product framing and instead treats every small part as part of one maintained field kit.

Prompt Extract

Use case: atlas-contact-sheet
Asset type: knowledge-base visual research final PNG
Primary request: a dense contact-sheet atlas of the small tools and consumables used by a summer pavement aquarium rental booth.
Scene/backdrop: neutral damp tabletop with faint chalk circles and clipped inventory tags.
Subject: hose nozzles, battery aerators, stone diffusers, dip nets, algae sachets, clip-on thermometers, stackable lids, snail shelters, water test strips, carry straps, rubber gaskets, and token tins.
Style/medium: catalog-specimen photography, practical and systematic.
Composition/framing: top-down contact sheet with 24 to 32 items grouped by function, even spacing but visible handling wear, no single hero object.
Lighting/mood: bright diffused daylight with small crisp shadows, matter-of-fact.
Color palette: pale aqua, mint plastic, coral nylon, galvanized steel, chalk white, olive algae, clear acrylic.
Materials/textures: wet mesh, ribbed hose rubber, brushed aluminum, pebbled plastic lids, clear blister-like containers, mineral residue.
Text (verbatim): tiny inventory numerals only if needed.
Constraints: coherent maintenance kit, no logos, no watermark.
Avoid: luxury flat-lay styling, fishing-gear branding, sci-fi lab hardware, too much empty space, glossy ad polish.
