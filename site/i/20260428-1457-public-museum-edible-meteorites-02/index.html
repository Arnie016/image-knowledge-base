Prompt Extract

Use case: photorealistic-natural
Asset type: public museum atrium interior
Primary request: a bright noon interior of the Public Museum of Edible Meteorites, with families and schoolchildren moving through a skylit central hall under hanging sugar-comet mobiles and brushed aluminum orbit rails
Scene/background: civic museum atrium with terrazzo floors, pale stone walls, rounded display islands, tasting counters, specimen vitrines, and a broad stair to upper galleries
Subject: a believable public institution built around edible meteorite exhibits, using charcoal candy specimens, amber glass shards, celadon teaching cards, chalk-cream signage panels, pale aprons, and restrained orange accession dots
Style/medium: documentary architectural photography
Composition/framing: wide eye-level interior frame with clear foreground display islands, busy midground visitors, and an airy upper-gallery background
Lighting/mood: honest noon skylight, soft reflections, educational and lively rather than theatrical
Color palette: terrazzo cream, stone gray, celadon paper, amber highlights, charcoal specimens, muted orange accents
Materials/textures: terrazzo, brushed aluminum, paper labels, sugar glass mobiles, polished handrails, worn museum flooring
Constraints: no logos, no watermark, no giant readable text, must feel like a real public museum instead of a theme park or luxury candy store
