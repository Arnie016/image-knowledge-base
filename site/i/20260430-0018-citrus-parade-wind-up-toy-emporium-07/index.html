Learning Value

Give the toy branch a human-scale social scene so it does not collapse into only packaging, plans, and object inventories.

Composition Notes

The demo loop dominates the frame to keep the image about shared handling and play, not just store decor.

Prompt Extract

Use case: photorealistic-natural
Asset type: knowledge-base visual research final PNG
Primary request: an in-store Saturday demo table where children and a clerk are winding and racing fictional citrus parade toys.
Scene/backdrop: old neighborhood toy shop interior, worn linoleum floor, low shelves, summer light coming through the front windows, hanging paper pennants.
Subject: one clerk in a green apron demonstrating how to wind a toy float while several children crowd around a tabletop parade loop with drums, balloon carts, and small bridges.
Style/medium: candid documentary photography with tactile period detail, grounded and unposed.
Composition/framing: medium-wide interior view from slightly above child height, demo table dominant, shelves and doorway visible behind.
Lighting/mood: strong afternoon sunlight with warm interior bounce, lively and communal.
Color palette: citrus brights against cream walls, honey wood, faded green apron, cobalt toy accents.
Materials/textures: scratched tabletop paint, scuffed toy plastic, cardboard boxes, polished tin wheels, paper streamers.
Constraints: fictional products only, no visible real brand logos, no watermark.
Avoid: contemporary big-box retail, perfect showroom minimalism, moody cinematic darkness, luxury fashion posing, dense readable signs.
