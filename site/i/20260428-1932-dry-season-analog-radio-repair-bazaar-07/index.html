Test whether the run can open outward into landscape scale while keeping the repair culture present as a domestic rooftop habit.

Composition Notes

The antenna silhouettes need to dominate so the roofscape reads as a social and technical forest, not generic city dusk.

Prompt Extract

Use case: environment-concept
Asset type: rooftop landscape documentary
Primary request: a dusk view across a neighborhood rooftop antenna forest above a dry-season analog radio repair bazaar
Scene/background: layered old rooftops with water tanks, clotheslines, small tuning sheds, patched parapets, and a dusty horizon softening into evening
Subject: clusters of handmade antenna masts, repair ladders, coiled wire baskets, one worker fine-tuning a mast, one family listening beside a repaired radio, and the bazaar lane glowing faintly below
Style/medium: documentary cinematic still with tactile realism, urban and wind-worn rather than romantic
Composition/framing: wide rooftop panorama with strong foreground mast silhouettes and deep city layering
Lighting/mood: amber dusk after a punishing hot day, quiet relief and technical attention
Color palette: smoke blue sky, faded teal paint, copper wire, chalk plaster, amber windows, dusty maroon cloth
Materials/textures: weathered concrete, bent metal rods, rope lashings, water tank plastic, dusty glass, sun-cured canvas
Constraints: no logos; no watermark; no futuristic skyline; no festival lights; keep it domestic, improvised, and believable
Avoid: sci-fi cityscape; glossy drone-shot tourism; neon night market styling; dramatic storm clouds; luxury rooftop lounge
