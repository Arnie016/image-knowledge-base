Learning Value

Test whether the branch can scale into retail architecture and public street atmosphere without losing its agricultural-trade specificity.

Composition Notes

The display needs one dominant bottle cascade and enough exterior rain reflection to situate it as a real street corner.

Prompt Extract

Use case: product-mockup
Asset type: storefront and installation view for a speculative fragrance retail branch
Primary request: a rainy evening storefront display for the Volcanic Orchard Perfume Exchange, with a cascading tower of bottles, fruit crates, blotter stones, and cooled vapor trays arranged behind a tall street-facing window
Scene/backdrop: narrow urban stone storefront at night with wet pavement reflections, rain on glass, and a glimpse of the interior beyond the display
Subject: sculptural bottle cascade, stacked orchard crates, smoked glass risers, clipped paper tags, dim tasting counter deeper inside, one or two passersby blurred outside for scale
Style/medium: editorial architectural photography, luxurious but inhabited, not a generic luxury perfume ad
Composition/framing: exterior three-quarter view from sidewalk level, window as the dominant plane, display composition rising diagonally upward inside
Lighting/mood: rainy night blue outside with warm amber interior display lighting and a little cool vapor glow around chilled trays
Color palette: wet slate, amber, olive, old rose, sulfur cream, smoky glass, oxidized brass
Materials/textures: rain-specked window glass, stone facade, bottle glass, vapor condensation, waxed paper tags, rough crates, brass trim, wet pavement
Constraints: original storefront only; no logos; no watermark; any visible text minimal and
