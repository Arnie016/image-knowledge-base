Test whether a page-driven theme can still produce a strong inhabited room without collapsing into generic library imagery.

Composition Notes

The room needed long depth and window-based orientation so the sea and ruined rides explain the map world without overt exposition.

Prompt Extract

Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: public reading-room environment
Primary request: wide interior view of a tidal library devoted to broken amusement-park maps, with tall cabinets of folded promenade charts, drying racks of repaired boardwalk diagrams, long public reading tables, and windows opening to a rough gray coastline and old pier rides beyond
Scene/background: upper-floor reading room above a seaside boardwalk archive, visible sea horizon, weathered railings, distant ferris-wheel skeleton and pier pylons outside the windows
Subject: the room itself as the main character, with readers and librarians only as sparse scale markers, map drawers, tissue-repair stations, brass desk lamps, hanging route fragments, and one large floor map under glass
Style/medium: cinematic environment concept art with tactile realism
Composition/framing: wide panoramic frame from one corner of the room, strong depth across tables to windows, architecture and map furniture clearly readable
Lighting/mood: storm-cleared afternoon light, silver sky, quiet public refuge, restrained atmosphere
Color palette: washed teal, cream, driftwood brown, brass, rain gray, faded coral accents
Materials/textures: salt-softened paper, painted wood, rippled glass, brass lamp stems, worn linoleum, canvas map tubes
