Use case: mixed-format
Asset type: album cover or archival record sleeve
Primary request: a square album-cover composition for the river conservatory archive, centered on one extraordinary hollow bone instrument resting upright against folded indigo cloth with shell charms, a brass ring, and a faint river horizon behind, designed as a photographed object-theater cover with minimal title space

Subject: one iconic instrument as the emblem of the conservatory's sound world
Style/medium: refined editorial album cover photography, quiet and tactile, not glossy music marketing
Composition/framing: strong centered hero object, symmetrical but gently imperfect supporting objects, square format with breathing room
Lighting/mood: pale dawn light, contemplative, restrained
Color palette: shell white, indigo, pale sky blue, muted brass, smoke brown
Materials/textures: bone pores, woven cloth, brushed metal, shell nacre, stone dust
Text (verbatim): "RIVER SONGS"
Constraints: keep text minimal and non-critical, no logos, no watermark, no modern microphone or speaker equipment
