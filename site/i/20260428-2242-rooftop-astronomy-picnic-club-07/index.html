Use case: infographic-diagram
Primary request: a dense handled teaching board used by the Rooftop Astronomy Picnic Club to orient beginners to the city night sky
Scene/background: physical board propped against a roof wall with clipped cards, hand-drawn skyline profile, moon path ribbons, and small sample charts rather than a flat digital graphic
Subject: orientation to rooftop directions, brightest seasonal stars, moonrise timing, simple etiquette reminders, and borrowing queue tokens rendered as physical cards and overlays
Style/medium: mixed-media educational board, tactile diagram photographed straight on, practical and inviting
Composition/framing: portrait board filling the frame with layered cards, acetate strips, and a hand-drawn horizon line creating strong hierarchy
Lighting/mood: even rooftop utility light with enough contrast to read shape layers, not polished studio light
Color palette: cream board, midnight blue cards, brick red accents, chalk white, brass fasteners, muted teal night-sky marks
Materials/textures: cork board, matte cards, acetate overlays, chalk pencil, cloth tape, pinheads, scuffed edges
Constraints: short non-critical text only; no logos; no watermark; must feel handmade, public, and easy to teach from
Avoid: flat classroom poster; generic science-center display; glossy corporate infographic; dashboard-like data viz
