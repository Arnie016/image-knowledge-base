Prompt Extract

Use case: atlas-contact-sheet
Asset type: final PNG for an image research knowledge base
Primary request: dense specimen atlas of biscuit zoology fair objects arranged like a playful natural-history study
Scene/background: cold cream tabletop with faint flour dust and a folded canal map partly visible underneath
Subject: many related objects in disciplined rows: iced animal biscuits, cocoa-dusted skeleton biscuits, label pins, tasting forks, enamel tins, flour sample envelopes, classification tokens, wool ribbon tags, tiny habitat cards, sugar-glass eggs, brass stampers, migration-arrow markers, specimen trays, and crumb brushes
Style/medium: tactile editorial catalog photography with museum-study precision
Composition/framing: top-down contact-sheet style with 24 to 30 objects, even spacing, a few overlapped paper sleeves, and clear object family logic
Lighting/mood: bright winter window light, crisp and handled rather than commercial food glamour
Color palette: flour white, ginger brown, cocoa black, sugared blue, brass, cranberry wax, wool gray
Materials/textures: iced dough, stamped paper, brushed tin, wool ribbon, crumb scatter, sugar crystals, matte ceramic
Text (verbatim): minimal tiny noncritical labels only if needed
Constraints: no logos, no watermarks, no bakery-ad styling, no candy-shop clutter, keep the objects taxonomic and civic
Avoid: glossy dessert magazine loo
