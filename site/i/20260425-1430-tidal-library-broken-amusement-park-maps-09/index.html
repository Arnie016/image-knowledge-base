Learning Value

Test whether the branch can close on a public coastal landscape that feels civic and alive instead of disaster-struck or nostalgic.

Composition Notes

The building needed to hold the frame as a real public institution while the ruined boardwalk and wet promenade explained the archive’s reason for existing.

Prompt Extract

Use case: photorealistic-natural
Asset type: exterior documentary landscape
Primary request: broad coastal exterior of the Tidal Library of Broken Amusement-Park Maps at blue hour after rain, perched beside a broken boardwalk with old ride structures and sea spray, warm interior lamps revealing map drawers and reading tables through tall windows
Scene/background: stone seawall, timber pier ruins, ferris-wheel frame and carousel roof in the distance, rough gray-green sea, wet promenade, gulls, tide pools
Subject: the archive building and surrounding public coastal landscape; only a few bundled visitors and staff as scale markers carrying map tubes and folded charts
Style/medium: photorealistic large-format documentary photo
Composition/framing: wide establishing frame from promenade level, building left-center, sea and ruined pier stretching right, reflections on wet ground
Lighting/mood: blue-hour afterglow, wet air, warm interior against cool coast, public and reflective rather than dramatic disaster
Color palette: storm blue, sea-glass teal, warm brass lamp light, cream windows, wet stone gray, faded coral signage accents
Materials/textures: wet concrete, salt stone, painted metal, rippled windows, canvas map tubes, weathered timber, puddles
Constraints: believable public architecture; no logos; no readable text required; no watermark; not post-apocalyptic; not luxury seaside
