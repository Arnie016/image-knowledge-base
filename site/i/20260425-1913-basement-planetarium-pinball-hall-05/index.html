Composition Notes

The cross-section keeps the quiet storefront, basement hall, repair rooms, and league backroom readable as one layered civic-mechanical organism.

Prompt Extract

Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: architectural section plate
Primary request: detailed cutaway section of a fictional basement planetarium pinball hall beneath an ordinary neighborhood storefront, showing the stair entry, coin desk, rows of celestial pinball machines, central star dome, parts cage, repair bench, mezzanine storage, and back-room league board
Scene/background: urban corner building in section, upper storefront modest and quiet, basement surprisingly dense with mechanical leisure infrastructure
Subject: the spatial logic of the hall and its support spaces, with believable machine spacing, stairs, ducts, shelves, stool rows, paper bulletin boards, and a compact projector dome
Style/medium: architectural illustration with tactile painted texture, halfway between a section drawing and a handcrafted exhibit plate
Composition/framing: full side cutaway across the building width, stacked levels readable left to right, no dependency on tiny labels
Lighting/mood: warm basement pools of light against cooler street-level dusk, cozy but workmanlike
Color palette: cream plaster, midnight blue accents, brushed metal, amber lamp glow, dusty wood, pale cyan projection arcs
Materials/textures: painted plaster, plywood, steel cabinets, chrome rails, velvet curtain, corkboard, wiring bundles, scratched coin counter
Constraints: no logos, no watermark, no futuristic spaceship
