Learning Value

Test whether a dense artifact wall can open a new street-performance branch without reading like a toy catalog or fantasy prop shop.

Composition Notes

The silhouettes needed to stay legible enough that the atlas behaved like a browsable object family instead of an overstuffed mural.

Prompt Extract

Use case: product-mockup
Asset type: artifact atlas
Primary request: photographed atlas of handcrafted storm-lantern puppets from a fictional underpass puppetry school, including 10 to 12 hanging puppet bodies and lantern frames such as rain herons, flood carp, gutter foxes, cloud drums, bridge moths, and lightning kites
Scene/background: soot-dark concrete wall partly covered by indigo tarps and chalked rehearsal marks, arranged like working parade artifacts rather than retail merchandise
Subject: many related puppets and lantern frames with a disciplined identity system, visible bamboo ribs, translucent waxed paper skins, patched silk, copper wire joints, stitched handles, rain scuffs, and a few tiny noncritical inspection tags
Style/medium: editorial documentary photography of handmade performance objects
Composition/framing: front-on wall atlas, dense but organized, each puppet readable as a distinct silhouette, strong negative shapes between them
Lighting/mood: damp dusk light mixed with warm sodium-vapor lamps, practical and slightly theatrical
Color palette: soot black, indigo tarp blue, amber lantern glow, oxidized copper, rain gray, one restrained safety-orange accent
Materials/textures: wet concrete, waxed paper, bamboo, silk patches, chalk dust, electrical tape, rust, puddle sheen
Constraints: no logos, no watermark, no copyrighted characters, no readable critical te
