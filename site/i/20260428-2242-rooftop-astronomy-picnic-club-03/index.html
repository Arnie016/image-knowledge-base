Composition Notes

Foreground blankets and charts anchor the picnic logic while the moon and skyline stay background witnesses rather than headline scenery.

Prompt Extract

Use case: photorealistic-natural
Primary request: a wide documentary night scene of a public astronomy picnic lesson on a city rooftop beside a water tower
Scene/background: flat apartment rooftop with blankets, folding stools, tea thermoses, chalk circles, low parapet, and a distant skyline under a clear spring sky
Subject: a volunteer teacher pointing a green-laser-free brass finder toward the moon while families and neighbors quietly look through binoculars and compare paper star maps
Style/medium: cinematic but plausible urban night photography, community event, not observatory marketing
Composition/framing: wide establishing frame with foreground blankets and charts, teaching circle in midground, skyline and moon in background
Lighting/mood: deep blue night, moonlit edges, dim practical roof lights, no harsh spotlighting
Color palette: ink blue sky, brick black, cream paper, ember red flashlight glow, brass, oatmeal blankets
Materials/textures: roof gravel, wool blankets, printed maps, thermos enamel, brass optics, chalk dust
Constraints: no telescopes that dominate the scene; no readable brand text; no watermark; must feel public, humble, and teachable
Avoid: luxury rooftop party; nightclub vibe; futuristic observatory tech; fireworks; romantic date framing
