Use case: typographic-layout
Asset type: event poster and ticket ephemera suite for a speculative public roller disco
Primary request: a styled flat-lay of a main event poster, ticket stubs, wristbands, checkerboard claim tags, matchbook-sized program, and small rule card from the sea-glass bathhouse roller disco
Scene/backdrop: cream tabletop with faint damp rings, clipped corners, and a few terrazzo chips nearby
Subject: printed ephemera arranged as a coherent civic event family with shell emblems, striped borders, bold geometric blocks, hand-stamped dates, and one dominant poster sheet
Style/medium: editorial print-artifact photography with disciplined graphic design, no need for perfect readable text
Composition/framing: top-down composition with the poster anchoring the center and smaller paper pieces radiating around it
Lighting/mood: soft afternoon light, tactile and slightly handled rather than pristine
Color palette: cream paper, sea-glass aqua, coral, navy, citron, faded chrome gray
Materials/textures: matte coated paper, stamped ink, thin card stock, fabric wristbands, tiny staple marks, slight corner wear
Text (verbatim): "ROLL", "GLIDE", "SEA-GLASS BATHHOUSE"
Constraints: make the text secondary to the graphic system; no logos; no watermark; keep it like a municipal leisure print world, not a corporate brand deck
Avoid: luxury invitation styling; wedding stationery
