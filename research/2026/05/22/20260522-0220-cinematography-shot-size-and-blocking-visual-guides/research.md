## What Each Image Should Teach

- `Shot Size Ladder Reference Poster`: compare extreme wide, wide, medium wide, medium, medium close-up, close-up, and extreme close-up with one actor and one room
- `Camera Angle And Eyeline Guide`: show eye level, high angle, low angle, over-the-shoulder, profile, and eyeline offset logic
- `24mm 50mm 85mm Lens Portrait Strip`: teach spatial distortion and compression through matched staging
- `180 Degree Rule Overhead Diagram`: show camera axis, actor positions, safe camera zones, and crossing-the-line failure state
- `Shot Reverse Shot Coverage Board`: show master, OTS left, OTS right, singles, and insert pickup logic for one dialogue scene
- `Living Room Blocking Floor Map`: show actor marks, camera positions, walking paths, and furniture obstacles from above
- `Apartment Dinner Scene Section Plate`: show camera heights, tripod positions, sightlines, practical lights, and window relation in cross-section
- `Director Viewfinder Tape Marks And Apple Boxes Atlas`: show the physical planning objects that make blocking concrete on set
- `Dinner Argument Storyboard Beat Page`: show how blocking and shot grammar translate into sequential scene beats
- `Rehearsal Blocking Wall Installation View`: prove the grammar pages can exist as a teaching and planning wall in a film classroom or indie preproduction room
- `Window Side Cinematic Proof Frame`: prove the same blocking logic can become a believable finished dramatic still
