## What Each Image Should Teach

- `Fragrance Family Wheel For Store Staff`: gives one at-a-glance map of the major family clusters and their repeated color and ingredient anchors
- `Eau De Parfum Versus Eau De Toilette Chart`: compares common concentration tiers through bottle fills, mist density, and testing-use context
- `Top Heart Base Notes Pyramid Guide`: explains top, heart, and base note logic without requiring long copy
- `How To Use Perfume Blotter Strips Poster`: shows a clean testing sequence with strip handling, spacing, and comparison method
- `Perfume Bottle Silhouette Comparison Board`: helps packaging and retail teams compare atomizer, cap, and bottle-body archetypes
- `Discovery Set Packaging Family Review`: proves how a coherent sample-kit family can look manufacturable rather than generic merch
- `Tester Strip Filing Ledger Reference`: gives retail educators a concrete archival object for organizing scent tests by family and date
- `Boutique Perfume Counter Testing Station`: places the physical testing objects in a believable retail context
- `Fragrance Advisor Training Wall`: shows how printed teaching surfaces, shelves, and tester objects coexist in staff onboarding
- `Scent Library Cabinet Section Cutaway`: turns the category logic into a spatial storage reference instead of another flat poster

## Successful Patterns To Chase

- Query-first titles using nouns like `wheel`, `chart`, `pyramid`, `poster`, `comparison board`, `testing station`, and `section cutaway`
- One dominant proof object per image so thumbnails remain readable
- Repeated physical hooks such as blotter fans, sample vials, resin chips, drawer tabs, and atomizer parts
- Restrained editorial finish with tactile materials instead of glossy beauty-ad polish
