## What Each Image Should Teach

- `Knot Types And Uses Training Chart`: matches recognizable knot forms to common marine tasks like loop, bend, hitch, stopper, and temporary securing
- `Bowline Versus Sheet Bend Comparison Board`: shows when a fixed loop differs from joining two lines of different tension or thickness
- `Cleat Hitch Step By Step Poster`: turns one dock-fastening action into a sequence that is readable at a glance
- `Standing Running Working End Rope Anatomy Plate`: explains rope-part vocabulary using a handled line rather than an abstract diagram
- `Rope Wear And Failure Warning Guide`: teaches visual warning signs like glazing, flattened braid, salt stiffness, cut sheath, and crushed eye splice
- `Waterproof Sailing Knot Field Cards Ring`: proves the branch can make portable teaching aids instead of only wall posters
- `Bosun Rigging Tools Tray Reference`: maps concrete hardware and maintenance objects that later prompts or agents can retrieve
- `Marina Dockside Knot Lesson Wall`: shows how a real teaching station organizes lines, practice cleats, charts, and damp working gear
- `Sail Locker And Line Rack Section Cutaway`: gives a less-common spatial planning format that explains line storage, tag bands, drying rails, and hardware bins
- `Wet Wharf Mooring Drill Proof Frame`: proves the same graphic language survives inside an active dock maneuver with people, rope handling, and rain-dark planks
