## What Each Image Should Teach

- `Power Transition Thesis Board`: shows the overall argument structure through actors, pressures, trigger points, and horizon markers
- `Maritime Chokepoint Pressure Map`: visualizes routes, narrow passages, queue pressure, insurance zones, naval shadow lines, and fuel dependencies
- `Rival Doctrine Comparison Sheet`: contrasts two strategic logics through objectives, tools, vulnerabilities, and favored terrain
- `Escalation Ladder Decision Tree`: explains how signaling, disruption, sanctions, convoying, blockade, and open conflict can branch from one another
- `Canal Blockade Timeline Board`: turns one chokepoint crisis into a turning-point sequence with forks, pauses, and compounding effects
- `Historical Analogy Comparison Plate`: compares multiple prior crises through route dependence, coalition shape, duration, and political legitimacy strain
- `Resource Corridor Vulnerability Dashboard`: shows flow variables, buffer stock, repair windows, convoy speed, and bottleneck concentration in one board
- `War Room Scenario Table`: proves the same analytical language works as a physical planning surface with tokens, acetate, and light cues
- `Dossier Folder And Signal Card Suite`: creates a handled artifact family for editors, teachers, or worldbuilders who need portable board assets
- `Night Convoy Chokepoint Proof Frame`: proves the abstract map logic can become cinematic storytelling without losing the same route and pressure cues
